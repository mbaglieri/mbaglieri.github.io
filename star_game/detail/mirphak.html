Alpha Persei (Î± Per) is the brightest star in the northern constellation of Perseus, just outshining the constellation's best known star, Algol. Known by the traditional names Mirfak and Algenib, it is a circumpolar star when viewed from the latitude of New York City or higher.[9] It has an apparent visual magnitude of 1.8,[2] placing it among the brightest stars in the sky. Mirfak lies in the midst of a cluster of stars eponymously named the Alpha Persei Cluster, or Melotte 20, which is easily visible in binoculars and includes many of the fainter stars in the constellation.[10] The distance to this star has been determined using the parallax technique, putting it 510 light-years (160 parsecs) away.[1]
