Alpha Crucis (α Cru, α Crucis, also Acrux, HD 108248) is the brightest star in the constellation Crux, the Southern Cross, and, at a combined visual magnitude 0.77,[6] is the twelfth brightest star[6] in the night sky. Acrux is the southernmost first-magnitude star,[6] just a bit more southerly than Alpha Centauri.
